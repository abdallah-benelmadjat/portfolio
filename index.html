About Me

I am a Ph.D. student and Research Assistant at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee, with a deep-seated passion for leveraging technology to solve complex energy challenges. My research focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence and mechanical engineering, specifically on optimizing wind turbine designs for noise reduction using genetic algorithms and deep neural networks.

With a Master's degree in Energy Engineering from Arizona State University and hands-on experience as an Energy Engineer at the U.S. Department of Energy's Industrial Assessment Center, I possess a strong foundation in both theoretical knowledge and practical application. I am driven by the goal of advancing sustainable energy solutions and shaping the future of Industry 4.0.

Currently, I serve as the President of the Association of Energy Engineers (AEE) student chapter in Milwaukee, where I lead initiatives to promote energy awareness and professional development among my peers.
