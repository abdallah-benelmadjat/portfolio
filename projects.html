AI-Driven Gear Fault Diagnosis

Academic Research Project | Jul 2021

Developed an advanced gear fault classification system using neural networks and deep learning. The system leverages signal processing and wavelet-based feature extraction from vibration data to accurately identify and diagnose faults in industrial machinery, enabling predictive maintenance.

Methodology:

  • Analyzed vibration data from a gearbox test rig with 6 distinct gear health states.
  • Engineered features using Maximal Overlap Discrete Wavelet Packet Transform (MODWPT) and entropy.
  • Trained a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) neural network for classification.
  • Implemented a 1D Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for end-to-end deep learning.

Key Achievements:

  • Achieved classification accuracy of 99.3% with the CNN model.
  • Successfully identified faults under 15 different operating conditions (speed and load).
  • Demonstrated the superiority of MODWPT over traditional LMD for feature extraction.
  • Developed a user-friendly visual tool to highlight the detected fault type on a diagram.

Game-Theoretic Energy Management for the Energy Internet

M.S. Graduate Project | Spring 2024

This project integrates game theory and big data analytics to optimize energy management in microgrids. It formulates the coordination between renewable and conventional energy sources as a three-stage Stackelberg game, using a deep learning-based algorithm for wind power forecasting to enhance system efficiency and stability.

My Contribution:

  • Developed the wind power prediction algorithm using a genetic stacked autoencoder (SAE) model.
  • Handled the programming and implementation in MATLAB to ensure the algorithm performed effectively.
  • Focused on optimizing the deep learning model to outperform similar forecasting techniques.
  • Analyzed the impact of forecasting accuracy on the game theory-based energy management model.

Key Project Features:

  • Stackelberg Game Model: Hierarchical modeling of the utility, storage company, and users.
  • Predictive Forecasting: A novel genetic SAE algorithm for accurate wind power prediction.
  • Dynamic Pricing: Optimal pricing strategies derived using backward induction.
  • Big Data Analytics: Leveraged data-driven techniques to inform energy management decisions.

Solar Panel Optimization and Modular PV Design

Square Solar Inc. | Fall 2023

The project focused on improving and prototyping a modular, ergonomic solar panel that addresses the growing need for compact, portable, and user-friendly solar technology. It specifically targeted small-space living applications such as RVs, boats, off-grid cabins, urban apartments, and emergency scenarios.

My Contribution:

  • Enhanced repairability, ease of installation, and durability through mechanical and structural redesign using SolidWorks.
  • Evaluated market potential, energy and environmental impact, and payback period.

Key Project Features:

  • Conventional solar panels are bulky, costly to repair, and vulnerable to localized damage.
  • The modular solution breaks the panel into smaller, replaceable units, allows DIY installation, and enhances durability while maintaining high energy output.

Methodology:

  • Created optimized 3D models of modular panels in SolidWorks.
  • Evaluated mechanical robustness, potential failure points, and improved mechanical connections for easy assembly.
  • Prototype features include lightweight, compact, interconnectable modules compatible with various mounting surfaces.

Key Achievements:

  • Cost: $1.2/W, significantly lower than conventional panels due to zero labor cost.
  • Energy savings: 12,556 kWh/year per household, with $1,574.5/year cost savings in Arizona.
  • Simple payback: 3.3 years for modular system vs. 6.4 years for traditional panels.

Environmental Impact:

  • 12,556 kWh/year energy production translates to 3,940 kg CO₂/year avoided emissions.
  • Modular panels outperform LG NeON 2 panels by ~10% in energy and CO₂ savings.

Use Cases & Applications:

  • Portable power for camping, boating, and RVs.
  • Apartment balconies and small rooftops.
  • Post-disaster recovery and emergency relief.
  • Scalable systems for off-grid communities.
